Knowledge and bias. Attitudes as leading indicators and compliance as lagging indicators.

 

  • Natural sciences a useful reference.
  • First thing in strategy is work out where you are in the predator cycle (hyena strategy!?)
  • Important to think about the generational body of knowledge in industries – IT ~4 generations, Aircraft ~6 Radiology is 3, medical >50 generations. More knowledge baked into the mature processes.
  • We do not see what we don’t expect to see and we cannot train ourselves NOT to do that…
  • After two or three interviews you often adopt a hypothesis and then you can only see evidence supporting it
  • Working with science, not against. Get good, real time data in a way that cannot be gamed and then you see the minority vs majority views.
  • Example of cyber security process.
  • Landscape mapping in culture exercises.
  • First map where things are, not where you want them to be.

Systems - ordered, complex and chaotic.

 

  • Ordered systems. Highly constrained. Operating theaters, leaving instruments in patients.
  • Example of IBM trying to break the "coffee / alcohol" cycle. Extreme energy waste to work around rules. Use of socratic technique. Appearance of order but it requires loads of good willed people for things to work.
  • Complex adaptive systems. Concept of modulators – retrospective coherence. Big difference from complexity.
  • Example of social media – they are not designed – they emerge.

Cynefin framework - a place of multiple belongings.

 

  • Analogy of riding a canoe down a fast flowing river.
  • Explanation of each domain, drawn by hand.
  • In the Obvious domain - (sense/categorise/respond; best practice; rigid/fixed constraints; if over-constrain, collapse into chaos)
  • In the Chaos (act / sense / respond)
  • In the Complicated domain (sense/analyse/respond; good practice; governing constraints)
  • In the Complex domain (probe/sense/respond in parallel; each idea gets small amount of resource/experiment what works; emergent practice; enabling constraints)
  • Difference between rules and heuristics. Napolean and marines. Concrete and tangible – not like “put the customer first” which can be re-interpeted. Commanders intent better than precise plans.
  • Next section is application of Cynefin to agile
  • Liminal areas (from anthropology). Waterfall in the Obvious domain is OK. Unanticipated needs – clusters of observational data upon which you can act. Not storypoints per se, raw stories. Use right tools for right process!
  • Scrum sits between complex and complicated. Things should not transition too quickly.
  • Specific examples of techniques being used, more comprehensive way of including users.
  • Tripple 8 method – good prototypers are normally bad operational code executors (different skill set) working across.
